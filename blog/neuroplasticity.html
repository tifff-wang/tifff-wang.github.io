Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

by Tiff Wang

Neuroplasticity:

• How understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people?

The term “neuroplasticity”, or we can call it “brain plasticity” for easier understanding, stands for the ability of our brains to reorganise and develop, both physically and functionally, through the experience of interacting with the external environment. People used to believe that only children’s brains are plastic, as they grow older, the plasticity faded and no new changes can be made to it. However, current research shows that at any age, brains remain plastic and can be significantly improved through getting Repetitive and intense training that drives a specific brain function can lead to enhancement of that function, even an injured brain can be recovered through such training.

Understanding the principles of neuroplasticity supports people to pay more attention to maintaining their brain health as they can actively engage in activities that promote cognitive well-being. It also motivates people to practice more when learning a new skill because they know that their ability to grasp new skills can be improved through practice and effort.

• How I engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for my benefit?

After learning about the concept of neuroplasticity, I feel highly motivated, especially in the context of learning coding languages. Firstly, it reinforces my belief that my coding skills can improve with consistent effort and practice. This mindset empowers me to embrace challenges and persist in my coding journey. Secondly, it encourages me to actively engage with the material while learning to code. Instead of passively following tutorials or code examples, I tend to practice through hands-on coding exercises, writing my own code, and experimenting with various solutions.

• What are some of the ways to increase my neuroplasticity?

First of all, to practice, practice, practice! With consistent practice the coding concepts through a series of relevant exercises, I can strongly feel that I tend to think more like a programmer now.

Additionally, pushing the boundaries and embracing challenges is crucial. Once I feel comfortable writing simple functions to solve basic questions, I actively seek out more complex scenarios, such as tackling the stretch challenges provided in the courses. By introducing variety and intensity into my coding practice and tackling diverse problems, I train myself to think critically and explore multiple solutions for the same problem.

Growth Mindset

• Discuss what it is and why it is relevant.

A growth mindset is the belief that you can improve your abilities and intelligence through effort and learning. It's about seeing challenges as opportunities to grow and understanding that your talents can develop over time. The concept was introduced by psychologist Carol S. Dweck in her book "Mindset: The New Psychology of Success." People with a growth mindset embrace challenges, learn from failure, and are inspired by others' success. Having a growth mindset boosts motivation, resilience, and a love for learning. It also encourages seeking feedback and viewing setbacks as chances for improvement, leading to personal and professional development.

• How I will integrate growth mindset into my learning journey?

When I initially started learning to code, I used to hesitate when it came to modifying functions that were already working or changing the code structure provided by the course. It reflected my fixed mindset. However, as I delved deeper into different concepts and methods, I realised that even if a function is functioning correctly, there might be a better way to write it. For instance, in my recent practice, I updated a previous function I had written by employing a chained high order function, resulting in cleaner code that enhances readability.

Additionally, at the beginning of my coding journey, I often became frustrated when I encountered obstacles or made mistakes. However, after familiarising myself with the concept of a growth mindset, I began to embrace these mistakes and setbacks. Instead of viewing them as personal failures, I now recognize them as valuable learning opportunities. I understand that making errors is a natural part of the learning process and can provide insights for improvement.
