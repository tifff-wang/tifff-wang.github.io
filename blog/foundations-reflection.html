JULY 13, 2023

My reflections for the foundation core learning.

by Tiff Wang

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

At the beginning of the course, I was initially reluctant to revisit my past due to the intense burnout from my previous career. However, through core learning, I mustered the courage to reflect deeply on my past experiences and identify the highlights of my capabilities.

Another valuable insight I gained about myself is a better understanding of my brain. The discussions on neuroplasticity during the sprint provided me with great motivation to practice coding on a daily basis and embrace challenges and mistakes. With this theory as a foundation, I noticed that my self-esteem has also grown as I've noticed a shift towards thinking more like a programmer through repetitive practice.

The third lesson I learned came from the exercises on listening and looping. Through these practices, I recognised my ability to be a good listener and show empathy. However, I also identified areas for improvement, particularly in expressing myself more effectively.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

I believe that in learning and programming, my values help me stay focused on my learning goals and prioritise what's important. When programming, my values of integrity encourage me to continuously improve the readability and maintainability of my code.

Empathy enables me to put myself in the shoes of users, understanding their needs to create a more user-centric outcome. For example, after finishing the sprint tech project exercises, I always think about what features can be added to make the product more intuitive. Additionally, empathy also helps foster communication and collaboration by actively listening to others and considering diverse perspectives.

Self-awareness helps me identify my strengths, weaknesses, and learning preferences. This awareness guides the tailoring of my programming learning plan, seeking resources that best support my learning style, and learning from mistakes to address areas that need improvement.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

What surprises me is that, initially, I thought my goal was solely to learn coding skills. However, as I followed the questions to formulate my learning plan, I realised that I have even higher expectations. Learning how to express my ideas confidently and collaborate effectively with others are also key skills I am eager to develop."

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

The most challenging aspect for me is probably the process of finding my values and identity. Looking back at my past, I made lots of mistakes in choosing my career path, which resulted in feeling totally lost and unsure of who I am. However, deep inside me, I know that I have the capability to achieve great things. Though it wasn't easy, by following the core reflection questions, I was able to discover who I am, what I believe in, and what my goals are.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I believe Dev Academy is providing us with a holistic education, enabling us to become programmers who can work both independently and collaboratively in teams. In our future careers, teamwork will be an essential aspect of project development, especially when companies adopt Agile methodologies. As programmers, we will not only communicate with fellow programmers but also with cross-functional teams such as designers, clients, and other stakeholders. Therefore, the core learning provides us with opportunities to learn effective communication skills, including the ability to articulate ideas, discuss technical concepts, and provide feedback, which are all crucial skills for being a good team player who contributes to enhancing the team culture.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead?

At the beginning of the course, I preferred tackling the tech challenges first, as I found answering the core questions much more challenging. After several sprints of core learning, I realised that this approach was beneficial for developing my self-esteem, strengthening my determination, and helping me identify my weaknesses that I should continue to focus on improving during the bootcamp.
