JUNE 22, 2023

An in-depth reflection on my values and identity.

by Tiff Wang

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

I encountered an ethical decision while working as a team leader in a childcare center. One of my team members consistently made grammar errors in her learning stories shared with parents, even after they were proofread. My manager emphasised that as a team leader, it was my responsibility to address the issue and prevent the teacher from repeating the same mistakes. However, the teacher is my good friend, who had a sensitive personality. After a thorough consideration, I decided to discuss the issue with her and provide constructive feedback, even though it led to her becoming very upset with tears. Later that evening, I wrote a long letter to her, explaining my decision and emphasising that it was driven by our collective goal of delivering high-quality learning records for the benefit of the children.

I feel that this ethical decision was an experience of balancing between leading the team towards professional excellence and managing personal relationships with team members. Previously, I was a very soft person who never dare to point out other people’s mistake or speak up when I have different opinion. However, as I gained valuable working experience with professionals and being more mature, I became more committed to the goals and developed the courage to communicate my concerns and providing constructive feedbacks.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

Prior to my arrival in New Zealand, I was raised in an environment where strict adherence to rules and procedures were emphasised from my early school years. However, my experiences in New Zealand have taught me the value of being open-minded and thinking critically from various aspects. In this culture, individuals are encouraged to share their opinions and actively identify gaps or areas for improvement in established procedures. Additionally, the concept of teamwork in New Zealand has had a great impact on me. Previously, I held the belief that individual capabilities determine the success. However,through my work experience in NZ, I have had numerous opportunities to engage in teamwork and collaboration, which has highlighted the importance of being a good team player and the positive impact it can have on achieving efficient outcomes.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

One of my strengths that greatly supports me during my learning journey is my strong commitment and motivation towards my career change. After going through a period of feeling lost and uncertain about my direction, I eventually discovered a clear purpose and goal to strive for. This has fuelled a strong determination within me. Whenever I faced with challenges, my strong commitment would push me forward to overcome difficulties and strengthen my dedication to learning and growth.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

I’m a artsy person which can be beneficial in design fields but I find it hinder my time management on coding practices. I tend to focus more and more on refining layouts and styles, which results in neglecting the initial goal of building a minimum viable product. This can lead to delays and challenges in meeting deadlines.

Another limitation is my hesitancy to engage in networking with people who work in the tech industry. Coming from an educational background, most of my connections are ECE teachers. I understand that networking is important in career development, especially landing the first job. But I still don’t have the courage to connect with professionals possibly due to lack of confidence in my technical skills.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why?

Based on my previous experience with teamwork, I have noticed that resistance and tension often arise when goals and rules are unclear, and when open communication is lacking. To address these challenges, I found that establishing clear goals and plans is crucial. Having a well-defined goal helps prevent misunderstandings among team members. Also using task management tools such as Trello boards can be an effective strategy to ensure that all team members are aware of the plan and each other's progress.

When tension happens, an open communication is essential. I usually like to create a safe space where team members can express their concerns, opinions, and ideas without fear of judgment. It helps us to find the root causes of tension. However, sometimes when the tension or conflict is too intense where personal emotions come into play, I feel that a proper calm break is neccesary rather than forcing the communication. I found it also works more effectively when invite a neutral person such as a facilitator to join the meeting to guide the team to solve the conflict.
